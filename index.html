My leader

NETAJI SUBHASH CHANDRA BOSE

About The Leader

  • Subash Chandra Bose was born on 3rd January 1897.
  • Subash Chandra Bose was a man of strong will power who greatly contributed to Indian independence.
  • He formed a Rani Jhansi regiment in his army, especially for the women led by Captain Lakshmi Sehgal.
  • On 4th July 1944, he gave the famous slogan Tum Mujhe Khoon Do Mai Tumhe Azadi Doonga.
  • Netaji was a prominent writer, and he started a newspaper Swaraj and was the editor of a newspaper named Forward.
  • He was also a master of disguise and deceived the British government on various occasions by his attire.
  • Ravindra Nath Tagore had first called him Netaji which later became his identity.
  • Netaji was a great legend who sacrificed his life and family for his motherland and died for his nation.
  • He believed that not with non-violence but India can get independence by a fight with the British government.
  • He visited various countries to ally with them to fight the British government.
  • Subhash Chandra Bose also started Azad Hind Radio in Germany to lead its independence movement on a global platform.
  • Netaji wrote the book The Great Indian Struggle to narrate the history of the Indian independence movement.
  • He got inspiration from Bhagavad Geeta and believed in the universal brotherhood.
  • The government released a coin of Rs. 75 in 2018 to commemorate Subhash Chandra Bose.
